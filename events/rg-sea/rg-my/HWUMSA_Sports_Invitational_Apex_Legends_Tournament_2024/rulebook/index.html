HWUMSA SPORTS INVITATIONAL APEX LEGENDS TOURNAMENT 2024 OFFICIAL RULEBOOK

Disclaimer: This Tournament is not affiliated with or sponsored by Electronic Arts Inc.

1. OVERVIEW

The HWUMSA Sports Invitational Apex Legends Tournament 2024 is organized by HWUMSA (Heriot-Watt University Malaysia Student Association).
The tournament is invitational and exclusive to universities in Malaysia (university registration fee is RM 30 per team upon invitation).
Target universities to be invited are as follows: Heriot-Watt, MMU, Cyberjaya, Nottingham, Southampton, Taylors, Monash, INTI, APU and Sunway.
The tournament spans over four days from March 7th - 10th (online).
The tournament is divided into two splits.
・Split 1 consists of 8 online games on the maps Broken Moon & Storm Point (March 7th - 8th, 2024).
・Split 2 consists of 8 online games on the maps Broken Moon & Storm Point (March 9th - 10th, 2024).
The tournament involves gameplay that will be broadcast over the internet.
The tournament will have a prize pool of RM 1,000.

2. ELIGIBILITY AND REGISTRATION

2.1 Registration Process and Deadlines

Invitations will be sent out from HWUMSA to the corresponding student associations and esports clubs of the selected universities.
Upon receiving the invitation, the university can then register their teams into the tournament via the registration form and with the administration of the tournament.
The registration fee is RM 30 per team (not refundable).
Universities can register up to two teams to participate in the tournament. HWUMSA reserves the right to change how many teams' universities can register depending on the responses as the tournament date nears.
HWUMSA reserves the right to change or remove the universities participating in the tournament.
Registration operates on a first-come, first-served basis and closes on March 4th, 2024 (12:00 PM).

2.2 Player Eligibility

Each player is required to meet the following criteria to participate in the tournament.
・Players must be the minimum age required to have a full (not underage) EA account in their territory of residence and meet the following age requirements at the time of registration.
・Players must be enrolled into a university that resides in Malaysia.
・Players must be able to show identification regarding the university they are enrolled in.

2.3 Team Eligibility

Each university team is required to meet the following criteria to participate in the tournament.
・All players on the team must be from the same university.
・Teams must consist of at least three players but no more than four players.
・One player from the team will be deemed the team captain. The team captain is responsible for managing the team and relaying all information from HWUMSA to the team.
・All players on the team must include the university they are representing in their gamer tag.

3.2 Team Communication

Teams and players must be in the official discord server throughout the tournament.
Teams and players must check in 20 minutes before each tournament day starts through the official discord server. Tournament check ins will start sharp at the indicated times in the tournament

structure.
Teams and players will be given a 10-minute break between matches to resolve any issues they may be facing. Matches will start sharp at the indicated times in the tournament structure.
Teams and players who fail to check in 20 minutes before the tournament or fail to present themselves before the start of a match will be automatically disqualified from the current match receiving a score of zero for that match.
Teams will be assigned their personal channel for voice communication. All players are required to play on their assigned channel during the tournament.

3.3 Scoring

At the end of each match, teams will be awarded points for their placement and total number of kills.
The points awarded in each match are:

Placement Points
1st 12
2nd 9
3rd 7
4th 5
5th 4
6th - 7th 3
8th - 10th 2
11th - 15th 1
16th - 20th 0
Kills 1 point for each kill

If a team misses a match, then the team receives a score of zero for that match.

3.4 Tiebreakers

In the event two or more teams are tied in Score, the tie will be resolved by the following:
Total Match Score: the Team with the highest total match score earned across all matches played in the tournament wins the tiebreaker.
If a tie breaker still persists then the tie breaker will be resolved by the following:
Total Kill Score: the Team with the highest total kill score earned across all matches played in the tournament wins the tiebreaker.

4. PLATFORM

Players may register and compete in the tournament on a supported platform using a single PSN account on the PlayStation 4 or PlayStation 5, a single Xbox account on the Xbox One or Xbox Series X|S, and a single EA account on PC.
The PSN account and Xbox account for their respective platforms may share a single EA account.

5. ENFORCEMENT

Teams and players may be disqualified immediately from the tournament at the discretion of HWUMSA for any of the following reasons:
・Using any cheats, hacks or other third-party “helper” applications in playing game matches.

・Using any configs, ximming and change of inputs in playing game matches.
・Intentionally disconnecting from the internet during any game match.
・Colluding with other teams/players in playing game matches.
・Taking advantage of known exploits in the game.
・Abusive or disorderly behavior, including any use of harassing, negative, or profane language online.
Moderators will be spectating the teams and players in game and on discord.
In the instance of technical difficulties, a server crash and or players not connecting to the server once the match has begun, HWUMSA, in its sole discretion, reserves the right to restart any match at any time and/or disqualify any player or team at any time for any reason, including but not limited to tampering with the entry process or the operation of the tournament.
In the case two or more teams report technical issues within one minute of the match start, a match reset will be issued. A maximum of 2 match reset will be issued for each split throughout the tournament.
Disqualification of a player may result in the disqualification of the player's entire team from the tournament.

6. PRIZES

The closing ceremony will be held online on March 10th (10:00 PM - 11:00 PM).

Potential winners will be required to complete and sign a prize winner declaration to claim their prize within 14 days (2 weeks) of the event's completion. If a potential winner fails to sign and/or return the prize winner declaration, refuses the prize, is ineligible to accept the prize, or the prize is returned as undeliverable, the potential winner forfeits the prize.
The tournament shall have a prize pool of RM 1,000.
RM 1,000 prize will be distributed to teams based on their finishing rank in the tournament. All prizes will be distributed equally among the team players who participated in the event via Touch n' Go vouchers.
The prizes for the tournament are as follows:

Finishing Rank Prize Amount Per Team
1st RM 400
2nd RM 300
3rd RM 200
4th RM 100
5th - 20th N/A
